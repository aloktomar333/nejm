The Clinical Reasoning Process Using NEJM Healer

Diagnostic reasoning is a multi-step iterative process, often opaque and difficult to convey.

NEJM Healer provides a way to explicitly teach and assess the different cognitive steps of clinical reasoning mitigating difficulties for educators and learners and improving diagnosis and management.

NEJM Healer demystifies clinical reasoning by combining knowledge (illness scripts and diagnostic schemas) with deliberate practice (via patient cases) in an interactive process that reinforces every learning step by making visible the expert rationale. By using the same proven process for each person every time, NEJM Healer makes developing clinical reasoning objective and provides measurement and feedback.

NEJM Healer Combines Knowledge with Deliberate Practice.

Only NEJM Healer offers expert-created, data-driven illness scripts and diagnostic schemas with highly interactive cases — all built on a foundation of current cognitive science illness script theory and dual process theory.

Users develop and refine skills by being able to compare their decisions with that of the experts. NEJM Healer provides an expert problem representation at each stage of the clinical encounter exposing what and how the experts think about each case, at every step of the process and explains their rationale behind it so that the learner can experience and absorb clinical reasoning while doing deliberate practice and getting expert feedback.

Getting consistent feedback against an expert helps educators meet the needs of learners with less resources (time, faculty and expertise) to create, assemble and teach the knowledge and reasoning. Also allows for consistent, objective feedback over a wide range of cases.

Gold-Standard Illness Scripts Created & Edited by Experts

NEJM Healer advances clinical reasoning by incorporating diagnostic probability ratios calculated using advanced data driven statistical models.

Experts engage in the case alongside the user, exposing them to expert inputs and objective formative assessment and feedback.

By the end of the case, in terms of what the student or resident can access for expertise, it's almost like having a half day with an expert, who's sitting down, taking you under their wing and teaching you one on one how they apply clinical reasoning.
